Here are some of our book recommendations

To Kill a Mockingbird

To Kill a Mockingbird is a novel by Harper Lee set in the American South during the 1930s. It explores themes of racial injustice, morality, and the loss of innocence through the eyes of young Scout Finch. The story follows Scout's father, Atticus Finch, a lawyer who defends a black man falsely accused of raping a white woman, as he confronts prejudice and hatred in the pursuit of justice.

1984

1984 is a dystopian novel by George Orwell set in a totalitarian regime where the government controls every aspect of life, including freedom of thought and expression. The story follows Winston Smith, a low-ranking member of the ruling Party, as he rebels against the oppressive regime and falls in love with Julia, a fellow dissenter. As they attempt to evade the watchful eyes of Big Brother, they discover the true extent of the Party's power and the consequences of rebellion.

The Great Gatsby

The Great Gatsby is a novel by F. Scott Fitzgerald set in the Roaring Twenties, a period of excess and decadence in America. It follows Jay Gatsby, a mysterious millionaire, as he pursues his lost love, Daisy Buchanan, and navigates the glittering but shallow world of the wealthy elite. Through the eyes of narrator Nick Carraway, the novel explores themes of love, wealth, and the American Dream, ultimately revealing the emptiness and disillusionment hidden beneath the facade of glamour.

Pride and Prejudice

Pride and Prejudice is a novel by Jane Austen set in early 19th-century England. It follows the Bennet sisters, particularly the intelligent and independent Elizabeth Bennet, as they navigate the social mores and expectations of their time in pursuit of love and happiness. The story revolves around Elizabeth's complicated relationship with the wealthy and aloof Mr. Darcy, as they overcome their pride and prejudices to find true understanding and mutual respect.

The Catcher in the Rye

The Catcher in the Rye is a novel by J.D. Salinger narrated by Holden Caulfield, a disillusioned and alienated teenager struggling to find meaning and connection in a world he perceives as phony and superficial. The story follows Holden's journey through New York City after being expelled from prep school, where he encounters various characters and confronts his own inner turmoil and existential angst.

One Hundred Years of Solitude

One Hundred Years of Solitude is a novel by Gabriel García Márquez that tells the multi-generational story of the Buendía family in the fictional town of Macondo, Colombia. Blending elements of magical realism with historical events, the novel explores themes of love, loss, and the cyclical nature of human existence. As the Buendía family experiences triumphs and tragedies over the course of a century, their lives become intertwined with the fate of their town, leading to moments of wonder, absurdity, and profound reflection.
